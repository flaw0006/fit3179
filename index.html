Visualizing Climate Variability Across Australia

Exploring Long-Term Patterns of Rainfall, Temperature, and Extreme Weather Across the Continent.

Average Annual Rainfall Across Australia

Australia’s climate is defined by sharp contrasts, from the humid tropics to the arid interior. This map illustrates the uneven distribution of average annual rainfall across the continent. Using a gradient of blue tones, it reveals how geography and prevailing weather systems shape regional precipitation patterns.

Darker shades indicate higher mean annual rainfall (mm). Hover over a state to view its average annual rainfall.

Rising Heat: Long-Term Temperature Trends Across Australian Cities

Five-year rolling averages of mean annual maximum temperature reveal persistent warmth in northern cities such as Darwin and Brisbane, while southern cities like Hobart and Melbourne remain cooler but are also trending upwards.

Rolling averages smooth out year-to-year variability, highlighting gradual warming across most cities since the 1990s. Darwin continues to record the highest long-term averages, with Brisbane and Perth following closely.

Temperature Extremes by State

Daily minimum and maximum temperatures across Australia reveal striking contrasts between regions. Warmer northern states such as the Northern Territory and Queensland experience higher overall maxima, while cooler southern regions record the lowest minima.

Dumbbell lines show the full observed range between daily minimum and maximum temperatures for each state or territory in 2024. Blue dots indicate coldest recorded minima, and red dots indicate hottest recorded maxima.

Extreme weather events per year

From bushfires and floods to cyclones and heatwaves, Australia faces a growing wave of natural disasters each year. This chart highlights the frequency of major extreme weather events since the early 20th century, showing how certain event types dominate particular decades and how disaster patterns shift alongside climate variability.

Each bar represents the total number of extreme weather events recorded across Australia in that year, coloured by event type.
